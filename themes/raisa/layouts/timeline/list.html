Timeline

A chronological overview of Raisa's journey and key events, as pieced together from her letters and diary.

April 14, 1942
Departs Kyiv. "В мгновенье когда тронулся поезд мое сердце остановилось." She is 18 years old.
~April 22, 1942
Arrives in Kraków. 1.5-day stop. Fed and given access to a bathhouse.
~April 24, 1942
Arrives in Ansbach. Placed in a city horse stable. Promised one night.
April 24 – May 14
21 nights in horse stable. Three delousing baths. Straw bedding. No information about where they'll go.
~May 15, 1942
Train to Würzburg. Registration and processing. "Куда — абсолютно неизвестно."
~May 8, 1942
Assigned to farm in Schönaich. Commandant gives them to an old farmer. "Komm bei mir." Separated from companion Raya. Meets Serbian worker Kurban. Begins diary.
May 9–10, 1942
First days of farm work. Cowshed at 4:55 AM. Cries all second night. Daily: cowshed → clover → weed beets → cowshed.
June 15, 1942
Earliest dated letter. Describes full daily schedule. Horses Pani and Ladi. "Hope to be home by winter."
September 1942
Learns to ride bicycle. Salary: 17 marks/month. Threshing with Serbian workers. Potato digging, plowing 5 hectares.
November 1942
Anna turns 22 — birthday card confirms she is Raisa's sister (born ~1920), not mother.
December 24, 1942
Christmas Eve. Sent 50 marks + 2 parcels home. Makes Ded Moroz (Grandfather Frost) for German children.
January 2, 1943
New Year diary entry. "Расставание" — separation. "Родина" — Motherland. Drawings in diary margins.
February 1943
Post-Stalingrad shift. "Masters suddenly fussing, soon we'll show them!" Wounded soldiers returning without arms and legs. "Germany (cursed)."
July 10, 1943
Eduard deploys to front via Frankfurt. Only 4 days home leave. Hedwig and mother pack him a parcel. Schön sits beside Raisa as she writes, wishes he could read Russian.
August 10, 1943
Air raid. 500+ bombers, 13 shot down. Visible 100 km away. Likely the Schweinfurt or Nuremberg bombing.
September 1, 1943
Transferred to Hannover. Factory work at Podbielskistraße 100. Shift-based labor. Friends with Vera and Mira.
May 9, 1944
Diary entry — one year before VE Day. Senses the Russians approaching. The war is turning.

Note: Some dates are approximate, reconstructed from letter contents, postmarks, and cross-references. The diary entries from May 1942 were written retrospectively.
